If you need help, get help.

If you are denied entry or detained by Customs and Border Protection (CBP) at an airport or other entry point please remember these points.

US Citizens

Questions

Answer all generic questions (where you were, purpose of the visit, profession, customs declarations, etc.) at primary screening at the border (the main booths that everyone goes to).

Secondary Screening

If you are sent to secondary screening (additional questioning besides the main booths), do the following and state 5 things:

  1. I am an American citizen.
  2. I have identified myself.
  3. I want to speak to a supervisor.
  4. I want a lawyer.
  5. I feel I am being religiously profiled.

Refuse

  • Refuse to answer any questions about your:
    • political views
    • religious practice or education
    (if you answer, they'll probably hold you longer)
  • Do not give your cell phone password.
    You do have to surrender your phone, but don't have to give your password.

Insist

Insist that they let you in. It is your right as an American citizen to be let into this country—no one can tell you otherwise.

When released

Immediately contact CAIR (Civil Rights Department) to file a complaint:

  • Email civilrights@cair.com
  • Call them at +1-202-742-6420

Green Card Holders

Resist

Resist any demands to give up your green card.

Do NOT Sign

Do NOT sign the Form I-407, even if they say it will make things easier. That form is for deportation!

Everyone

Demand

A hearing from an immigration judge. Do NOT relinquish this right.

An attorney as you have the right to representation. Ask to speak to an attorney and discuss details with them and NOT CBP.

Obtain

Names and badge/worker numbers of everyone you talk to.
